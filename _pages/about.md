I am an M.Sc. student in the Graduate Program in Production Engineering and Systems (PPGEPS) at Pontifícia Universidade Católica do Paraná (PUCPR), Brazil. My academic background is in Control and Automation Engineering, with a strong interest in robotics, dynamic systems and experimental research in control.

My research focuses on the development and analysis of robotic systems, particularly systems with unstable dynamics such as wheeled biped robots and two-wheeled inverted pendulum platforms. These systems are studied both from a theoretical perspective, involving modeling and control design, and from an experimental perspective through the development of physical prototypes and test platforms.

My work also explores the use of computational tools to extract and analyze experimental data from physical systems. Recently, I conducted a study using computer vision techniques to obtain kinematic data from experimental videos through DeepLabCut, a deep learning-based markerless pose estimation framework. This work focuses on extracting motion variables from a Ball and Beam experimental setup in order to support the modeling and analysis of dynamic systems. A manuscript describing this study is currently in preparation.

My broader research interests include:

- Robotics and mobile robotic systems  
- Dynamic balance and control of unstable systems  
- System identification and modeling of nonlinear systems  
- Computer vision applied to experimental analysis  
- Learning-based approaches for modeling and control of physical systems
